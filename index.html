Motivation

Community Servings is a nonprofit food and nutrition program located in Jamaica Plain, Massachusetts. The organization provides medically tailored and nutritious meals to chronically and critically ill individuals as well as their families. As an organization, they are committed to prioritizing racial and economic justice and health equity. As such, Community Servings actively engages the healthcare and policy leaders to advocate for the integration of medically tailored meals into the healthcare system. At their core, Community Servings believes that food not only provides sustenance, but is also a source of community, of an agent of change, and most importantly, of healing.

In partnering with Community Servings, our goal is to provide interactive visualizations to effectively characterize the organization's clientele. Our project will communicate information about the clients' geographical, demographic, and contract distributions. Additional insights such as success rates amongst different routes will be explored as well.

Data

Each entry in our dataset is a delivery/attempted delivery by Community Servings in the year 2022. Salesforce was used to collect and store the data. The information given for each entry includes delivery details, client demographics, and contract types. See the following for the data types.

  • Total meals - quantitative
  • Route - categorical
  • City - categorical
  • County - categorical
  • State - categorical
  • Zip - categorical
  • Age - quantitative
  • Gender - categorical
  • Race/ethnicity - categorical
  • Contract Type - categorical
  • Client status - categorical
  • Delivery status - categorical

Task Analysis

Our team interviewed Adam and Kate from the data analytics team at Community Servings. During introductions, we learned that Adam had a background in public health and epidemiology, and that he started out as a volunteer. He emphasized that the mission of Community Servings is to treat food as medicine and to use it as a preventative measure against illness. As we began discussing the data, we learned that they collected all of their delivery route data for 2022 through SalesForce. The dataset includes delivery status, delivery route, zip code, number of meals in the delivery, as well as client demographics. Given this information, they wanted us to map out where their deliveries are going to, whether these deliveries were successful, and track the client demographics. Other than these specific questions, they strongly urged us to investigate any unknown relationships between the data and encouraged us to take a more exploratory approach. Additionally, Adam and Kate mentioned that they plan to present the visualizations that we will create in their annual financial report. If certain areas were discovered to have a very dense delivery history, it's possible that our visualizations could help justify additional funding to open a new location.

A second brief interview was conducted to clarify the team goals with Adam. It was established that an additional dataset including unique client IDs was needed to proceed. Rather than mapping out the number of deliveries in a region, it was requested that we map out the number of clients instead. It was also suggested for us to visualize the distribution of contract types in each region as well. The new dataset will also eliminate duplicate data in client demographics caused by multiple deliveries to the same client. As such, the task analysis table has been adjusted to reflect the new goals.

Design Process

This visualization is a choropleth map that aims to answer both tasks 1 and 2. The map shows the geographical spread of the deliveries, and the stacked bar chart shows the demographic information for each zip code. The choropleth map uses the mark of area and the channels of position and color. The position represents boundaries of each zip code within Massachusetts, and the colors represent the density of deliveries in that zip code. The stacked bar chart uses the mark of lines and the channels of position, color, and size. The x-axis represents the age groups of the clients, and the y-axis represents the number of deliveries. The colors represent different race and ethnicity groups, and the lengths represent the number of deliveries for each age group.

The choropleth map is our main chart, but once a user clicks within a zip code, the stacked bar chart will pop up. This is the first interactive element. Ideally, this will be a two-way link. Clicking on a race/ethnicity in the legend will show only the deliveries for that demographic on the choropleth map.

In addition, another chart that shows the contract type breakdown will be added as requested by our partner. This visualization will be a “details-on-demand” feature of our main map and will be shown alongside the client demographic breakdown after clicking within a region. This chart will be a simple bar chart that utilizes dynamic features such as tooltips.

Because one of our tasks involved the geographical spread of client deliveries, we knew a map was necessary. There were two maps that we were deciding between: a dot density map and a choropleth map. After careful consideration, we chose the choropleth map because a dot density map may have large dots that overshadow other dots. Another one of our tasks involved the demographic of the clients. We originally chose a layered histogram with age as the binned variable and race as each individual histogram. However, the age data we were given were ordinal (e.g. 0-9, 10-19..) rather than quantitative, so we moved away from the histogram. In addition, some race/ethnicity groups were only represented in certain age groups, so the layered histogram was not a great way to visualize the demographic breakdown. Thus, we chose the stacked bar chart to showcase this data.

Second Visualization: Total Meals Delivered by Route Distance

Both sorted bar charts above show the same information, however, the first chart omits the outlier of the shipped route to allow for better viewing of the rest of the routes. In the final visualization, we will use the graph without shipped as shipped implies that Community Servings used another service to deliver their meals and is of less interest to them, then deliveries their employees carried out. This chart uses line as the mark while length and color are the channels used to encode number of deliveries and average route distance respectively. Average Rout distance was calculated using the latitude and longitude of Community Servings and the delivery zip code coordinates. From this chart we can see that the routes with the most deliveries tend to be shorter routes and the longest distance routes have less total meals delivered.

Third Visualization: Client Demographic Breakdown

This is a stacked histogram of the different racial demographics of a selected region of Massachusetts. The mark is, of course, a line, while channels include length (how many people of each demo are represented) and color (to differentiate the demos visually), though color is not encoded into the chart itself (as race is a nominal attribute).

Now, as for the results: it makes sense. Those at the midway point of their lives are going to be the most likely to not have any other form of social assistance they need to access consistent meals. For kids and teens, school breakfast and lunch is usually an outlet, and those who are older are given medical assistance (not good assistance, but more than nothing). So Community Servings steps into the 40 to 70 range to provide meals to those who can't get them otherwise. Racial demographics are not surprising either, as the top 3 racial groups in Boston, Black, White, and Latinx, are the top 3 here too. It's also sadly not surprising that Black and Latinx people are overrepresented here, as they are also the groups more likely to be in poverty than their White peers.
