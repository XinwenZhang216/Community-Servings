Dhruv Gandhi, Eli Paul, Ezra Statsky-Frank, Xinwen Zhang

Motivation

Community Servings is a nonprofit food and nutrition program located in Jamaica Plain, Massachusetts. The organization provides medically tailored and nutritious meals to chronically and critically ill individuals as well as their families. As an organization, they are committed to prioritizing racial and economic justice and health equity. As such, Community Servings actively engages the healthcare and policy leaders to advocate for the integration of medically tailored meals into the healthcare system. At their core, Community Servings believes that food not only provides sustenance, but is also a source of community, of an agent of change, and most importantly, of healing.

In partnering with Community Servings, our goal is to provide interactive visualizations to effectively characterize the organization's clientele. Our project will communicate information about the clients' geographical, demographic, and contract distributions. Additional insights such as success rates amongst different routes will be explored as well.

Data

Each entry in our dataset is a delivery/attempted delivery by Community Servings in the year 2022. Salesforce was used to collect and store the data. The information given for each entry includes delivery details, client demographics, and contract types. See the following for the data types.

  • Total meals - quantitative
  • Route - categorical
  • City - categorical
  • County - categorical
  • State - categorical
  • Zip - categorical
  • Age - quantitative
  • Gender - categorical
  • Race/ethnicity - categorical
  • Contract Type - categorical
  • Client status - categorical
  • Delivery status - categorical

Task Analysis

Our team interviewed Adam and Kate from the data analytics team at Community Servings. They emphasized that the mission of Community Servings is to treat food as medicine and to use it as a preventative measure against illness. As we began discussing the data, we learned that they collected all of their delivery route data for 2022 through SalesForce. The dataset includes delivery status, delivery route, zip code, number of meals in the delivery, as well as client demographics. Given this information, they wanted us to map out where their deliveries are going to, whether these deliveries were successful, and track the client demographics. Other than these specific questions, they strongly urged us to investigate any unknown relationships between the data and encouraged us to take a more exploratory approach. Additionally, Adam and Kate mentioned that they plan to present the visualizations that we will create in their annual financial report. If certain areas were discovered to have a very dense delivery history, it's possible that our visualizations could help justify additional funding to open a new location.

A second brief interview was conducted to clarify the team goals with Adam. It was established that an additional dataset including unique client IDs was needed to proceed. Rather than mapping out the number of deliveries in a region, it was requested that we map out the number of clients instead. It was also suggested for us to visualize the distribution of contract types in each region as well. The new dataset will also eliminate duplicate data in client demographics caused by multiple deliveries to the same client. As such, the task analysis table has been adjusted to reflect the new goals.

Design Process

Our original goal was to create a choropleth map that aims to answer tasks 1 and 2. The map will show the geographical spread of the clients, and a linked stacked bar chart will show the demographic breakdown for each zip code. However, during our implementation, we discovered that zip codes aren't confined to distinct boundaries, so we expanded our regions to counties. In addition, due to the complexity of building choropleth maps for five different states, we were unable to create the interactivity using Altair. To compromise, the final map shows only Massachusetts, which accounts for the majority of Community Servings deliveries.

While we were given information about the clients' age, gender, and race, our visualization was confined to two dimensions, meaning we had to choose one of these categories to omit, or to include one using an interactive element. We decided to display the clients' age and race in a stacked bar chart, with the option to select a gender in a dropdown bar. However, with the amount of interactivity in this chart, we felt like it would be overwhelming to combine this chart with our interactive map. Therefore, we decided to keep the demographic visualization as its own standalone chart.

Because the demographic chart was now a standalone chart, we needed a chart to link with the map. At this time, a new task was given to us by our partner, which was to characterize the breakdown of contract types within each region. Similar to the original idea, clicking within a county will show the contract type distributions within that county.

Our last chart takes an exploratory path to discover any relationships between the routes and the meals delivered, as well as how far these deliveries are.

Data Visualization(s)

First Visualization: Client Density by County with Contract Type Breakdown

This visualization is a choropleth map showing the density of Community Servings clients per county in Massachusetts. Clicking within a county shows the breakdown of the contract types that the clients in the selected county hold. This map also allows for multiple selection - clicking while holding shift will show the data for multiple counties.

It appears that the major contract type is Medicaid ACO, closely followed by philanthropic, then government contract. It's unsurprising that ACOs account for a high number of contracts as ACOs are encouraged or even required by many states to address social drivers outside of medical care, such as food security and nutrition, often through partnerships with community-based organizations.

Within Suffolk County, however, philanthropic contracts make up the highest number of contracts held by clients, and the county also consists of the highest number of government contracts out of the counties analyzed. The high density of clients within Suffolk County may have contributed to this, but another factor may be the increased diversity in client demographics as well.

Second Visualization: Total Meals Delivered by Route

The sorted bar chart above show the total meals delivered by route. There are two dropdown bars that the user is able to interact with. The first dropdown allows the user to select whether to include the outlier "Shipped", which signifies meals delivered via UPS.For routes named “X”, if the contract type is Home Hospital, then the meals were delivered by the Community Servings Delivery Team. If contract type is Government or Philanthropic, then they were delivered by a volunteer. Another dropdown allows the user to select the routes based on days of the week, which were used by Community Servings to categorize the routes.

In addition, the color of the bars represent the average route distance by miles. This was calculated using the latitude and longitude of Community Servings and the delivery zip code coordinates. From this chart we can see that the routes with the most meals delivered tend to be farther, and vice versa.

Third Visualization: Client Demographic Breakdown

This is a stacked bar chart of the different racial demographics of a selected region of Massachusetts. The results are unsurprising. Those at the midway point of their lives are going to be the most likely to not have any other form of social assistance they need to access consistent meals. For kids and teens, school breakfast and lunch is usually an outlet, and those who are older are given medical assistance (not good assistance, but more than nothing). So Community Servings steps into the 40 to 70 range to provide meals to those who can't get them otherwise. Racial demographics are not surprising either, as the top 3 racial groups in Boston, Black, White, and Latinx, are the top 3 here too. It's also sadly not surprising that Black and Latinx people are overrepresented here, as they are also the groups more likely to experience food insecurity than their White counterparts.

Conclusion

In this project, we created four different visualizations: a choropleth map that shows the client density by county in the state of Massachusettes, a stacked bar chart that is linked to the map which shows the contract type distribution within the selected region, an interactive bar chart that displays the total meals delivered by the route used as well as route distance, and finally, an interactive stacked bar chart that shows the client demographics.

Through these visualizations, we addressed all of our given tasks, with the exception that client demographics weren't analyzed within specific counties as originally planned. Additionally, our approach to task 4 was more indirect, as we posited that greater number of meals delivered would correlate with greater success rate of the route/driver.

While we were able to address our tasks, there are several improvements and room for future work. For example, Altair was unable to create an interactive choropleth map with more than one state, which was the biggest problem that we faced. Future works could utilize more advanced libraries such as D3 to create visualizations that Altair was unable to produce. Depending on the usability by the partnering organization, the demographic chart could be altered to encode only the age and race, so that it can be linked to the map along with the contract type breakdowns.
